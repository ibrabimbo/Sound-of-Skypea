The shown demonstration shows a panel of the parachute, which you will need two of, to make it a whole (seen in the equation).

**The measurements:**

*a:* 25 cm  
*x:* 75 cm  
*y (diameter of hole):*  20 cm

**1\.**

* Take your cloth and cut out two identical panels based on the instructions. I would recommend using a scissor but whatever feels comfortable and does the job is a great alternative.   
* *The dotted lines do not represent cutting lines, they are only there to visualize the 3:1 ratio of the sides of the rectangle.*




 ![Logo](https://github.com/ibrabimbo/Sound-of-Skypea/blob/main/Pictures/parachute2.png) 

 
**2\.**

* After you cut out the 2 panels, lay them on top of each other, with a 90 degree relative rotation, like you see in the picture. The two holes should perfectly overlap each other, so that you are able to see your working surface through the panels holes.   
* The ***x*** marks in the picture represent the lines that you should sew, follow these while the cloth is running through your machine, or when you are sewing by hand.   
* It is important that when sewing, you pay attention that each of your stitches actually goes through both panels, rather than just one.   
* Try to leave about 2mm of cloth between your sewing line and the very edge of the panel.  
*  If needed, you can prepare by fixating this position with needles and only sewing one side at a time. I would start with the sides of the “square” in the middle, and then sew together the outline of the circles. Use your most frequent linear setting on the machine, or sew accordingly by hand.

 ![Logo](https://github.com/ibrabimbo/Sound-of-Skypea/blob/main/Pictures/parachute3.png) 

 
**3\. Strings**  
 ***The blue lines represent the strings.***

* After you have sewn the two panels together, lay it out flat.   
* Take you string and cut 4 pieces, each 75 cm long. Then lay the pieces of string on top of your parachute like in the image, pay attention to leave about 2mm between the string and the very edge of the cloth.  
*  Position the strings so, that they are parallel to the sides of the parachute and overextend it by 25cm on each side. Then mark the points which are shown with ***x***  marks in the image on the strings. These are the points where the cloth should meet the string.   
* After this, start sewing the strings onto the parachute, one by one. Align the marks which you made earlier with the edge of the cloth, then start stitching. Use a zigzag pattern, and pull it real tight so that it holds the strings in place.
